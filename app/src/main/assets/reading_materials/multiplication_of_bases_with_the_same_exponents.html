Multiplication of Bases with the Same Exponents


Mulitiplication property bm ⋅ bn = bm+n

When you multiply two numbers or variables with the same base, you add the exponents. This rule does not hold if the numbers are of a different base.


The following three examples show why these property is true for any positive integer exponents:


• b3 ⋅ b3 = (b ⋅ b ⋅ b) ⋅ (b ⋅ b ⋅ b) = b6
Multiply the base three times because the exponent is 3 and add the exponents

• 22 ⋅ 32 = 4 ⋅ 9
Simplify 22 by multiplying repeatedly based on the exponent: 2 ⋅ 2 = 4 multiply by 32 by multiplying repeatedly based on the exponent: 3 ⋅ 3 = 9
= 4 ⋅ 9 = 36
Then, multiply the result of the first step : 4 * 9.

• 32 ⋅ 32 = 32+2
If the base is the same, copy firs the same base then add the exponent
= 34 = 3 ⋅ 3 ⋅ 3 ⋅ 3 ⋅ = 81
To simplify, multiply the base repeatedly four times because the exponent is 4 by the result on the first step.
