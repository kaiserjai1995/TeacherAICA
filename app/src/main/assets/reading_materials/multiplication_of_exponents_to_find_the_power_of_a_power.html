Multiplication of Exponents to Find the Power of a Power

If n and m are positive integers and a is a real number, then (an)m = anm

Because the proof of this theorem requires mathematical induction, we give an informal argument instead:
(an)m = an • an • an ••• an (m factors of an)
            = an+n+n+ ... +n (m terms of n in the exponent)
            = anm

Examples: 

	a. (54)2
	b. (x2)4
	  (y5)4
	c. [(2 + b)2]3
	d. (x3 • x4)2

  a. (54)2 = 5(4)(2) = 58 or 390,625
