Multiplication of Exponents to Find the Power of a Power

If n and m are positive integer and a is real number, then (an)m = anm

Because the proof of this theorem requires mathematical induction, we give an informal argument instead:
(an)m = an • an • an ••• an (m factors of an)
            = an+n+n+ ... +n (m terms of n in the exponent)
            = anm



Examples: 

	a.(54)2
	b.(x2)4 
	  (y5)4 
	c. [(2 + b)2]3 
	d. (x3 • x4)2
	e. [(x5 • x3)3 ]2
	f. 2x2 • (5y2)2

Solution:


a. (54)2 = 5(4)(2) = 58 or 390,625

Just multiply together the exponent and just copy the base. Also, simplify your answer.




Just multiply together the exponent and just copy the base.



c. [(2 + b)2]3 = (2 + b)6 = (26 + b6) = 64 + b6

Just multiply the exponent together. Then simplify the equation by distributing the exponent (6) to the 2's and b's. To get the simplest form, multiply 2 according to the exponent and just copy the variable with the exponent.



d. (x3 • x4)2 = (x7)2 = x14

The first thing is to solve whatever inside the parenthesis, just add the exponent and copy the variable. After it, just multiply the exponent.



e. [(x5 • x3)3 ]2 = [(x5+3)3]2 = [(x8)3]2 = [(x8•3)]2 = [x24]2 = [x24•2] = x48

The first thing is to solve whatever inside the parenthesis, just add the exponent and copy the variable. Then, multiply the exponent inside the square bracket. And lastly, multiply the exponent outside the bracket to get the final answer.



f. 2x2 • (5y2)2   = 2x2 • 52y2•2  = 2x2 • 25y4  = 2 • x2 • 25 • y4   = 50x2y4

Distribute the exponent 2 to the coefficient and variable y. The variable y has its own exponent so you need to multiply it exponentially from the distributed exponent. Then, you can now multiply the coefficients and their variables.
