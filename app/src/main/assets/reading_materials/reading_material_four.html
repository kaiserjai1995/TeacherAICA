Multiplication of Bases with the Same Exponents


Mulitiplication property bm ⋅ bn = bm+n

The following three examples show why these property is true for any positive integer exponents:

• b3 ⋅ b3 = (b ⋅ b ⋅ b) ⋅ (b ⋅ b ⋅ b) = b6
Multiply the base three times because the exponent is 3 and add the exponents

• 22 ⋅ 32 = 62+2
Multiply the two bases and add the exponents
= 64 = 6 ⋅ 6 ⋅ 6 ⋅ 6 = 1296
To simplify, multiply the base repeatedly four times because the exponent is 4

• 32 ⋅ 32 = 92+2
Multiply the two bases and add the exponents
= 94 = 9 ⋅ 9 ⋅ 9 ⋅ 9 ⋅ = 6561
