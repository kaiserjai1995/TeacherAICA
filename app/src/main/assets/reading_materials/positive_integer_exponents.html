Positive Integer Exponents

In this section, we will develop the basic properties used to manipulate negative integer exponents.


• Write an exponential expression without exponents

We use natural-number exponents to indicate repeated multiplication. For example.

25 = 2 ⋅ 2 ⋅ 2 ⋅ 2 ⋅ 2 = 32
73 = 7 ⋅ 7 ⋅ 7 = 343
x4 = x ⋅ x ⋅ x ⋅ x
y5 = y ⋅ y ⋅ y ⋅ y ⋅ y

These examples suggest a definition for xn, where n is a natural number.

If n is a natural number, then xn = x ⋅ x ⋅ x ⋅ ⋅ ⋅ ⋅ ⋅ x

In the exponential expression xn, x is called the base and n is called the exponent. The entire expression is called the power of x.

Base →xn ← Exponent

Example: Evaluate the following, 24 , 25 and 33.

SOLUTION 
	
• 24
	= 2 ⋅ 2 ⋅ 2 ⋅ 2
	= 16
	
• 25
	=  2 ⋅ 2 ⋅ 2 ⋅ 2 ⋅ 2
	= 32
	
• 33
	= 3 ⋅ 3 ⋅ 3
	= 27

If an exponent is a real natural number, it tells how many times its base is to be used as a factor. An exponent 1 indicates that its base is to be used one time as a factor, an exponent of 2 indicates that its base is to be used two times as a factor, and so on.
